Key Facts About Montana

Attribute Description Interesting Fact
Population 1,005,141 Montana ranks 44th in the U.S. by population.
Nickname Treasure State The nickname comes from the wealth generated by its mining industry.
State Capital Helena Helena was once home to more millionaires per capita than any other city in the world.
State Flower Bitterroot The bitterroot is edible and was a traditional food source for Native Americans.
State Bird Western Meadowlark Known for its distinctive and melodious song.
State Animal Grizzly Bear Grizzlies are a symbol of wilderness and strength.
State Fish Blackspotted Cutthroat Trout Native to the cold-water tributaries of the Pacific Basin in North America.
State Gemstones Sapphire & Agate Montana sapphires come in a variety of colors, unlike those from any other location.
State Soil Scobey Soil Scobey Soil covers over three million acres of Montana land.
State Motto Oro y Plata (Gold & Silver) The motto reflects the importance of mining to Montana's history and economy.

Hisory of Montana

Montana's history is rooted in the rich heritage of Native American tribes, followed by the arrival of Lewis and Clark and the gold rush of the 1860s, which led to statehood in 1889. Conflicts like the Battle of the Little Bighorn and the rise of the influential Anaconda Copper Company shaped the state's early years. Post-World War II, Montana transitioned from resource extraction to a service-based economy, with tourism surpassing mining as the second-largest industry. Despite economic fluctuations, Montanans are known for their strong community spirit and deep connection to the environment. Summarized from mt.gov/discover/brief_history.
